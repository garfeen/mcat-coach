MCAT 515+ Elite High-Yield Principles

01

Active Recall Over Passive Reading

Do not spend hours reading study guides. Test yourself continuously. Sketch out biochem pathways (glycolysis, TCA) from memory.

02

CARS Passage Mapping

For CARS, read for the author’s primary thesis and tone rather than facts. Spend 3.5 minutes reading and 6 minutes answering.

03

Master Dimensional Analysis

In physics and chemistry, if you forget an equation, align units (e.g. Joules = kg*m²/s² = N*m = C*V). This is a lifesaver for hard calculations.

04

Analyze Every Practice Error

Log all errors. Note why the correct choice is correct and why the chosen option is wrong. Memorizing concepts is not enough; learn the test structures.

Biochemistry & Gen Chem

Michaelis-Menten Equation
v = (Vmax * [S]) / (Km + [S])
Relates reaction rate (v) to substrate concentration ([S]). Km is the substrate concentration at 1/2 Vmax. Lower Km indicates higher affinity.
Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation
pH = pKa + log([A-] / [HA])
Used to calculate pH of buffer systems. When weak acid concentration [HA] equals conjugate base [A-], pH = pKa.
Gibbs Free Energy Spontaneity
dG = dH - T*dS
Determines reaction spontaneity. dG < 0 is exergonic/spontaneous; dG > 0 is endergonic/non-spontaneous.

Physics Principles

Thin Lens Equation
1/f = 1/do + 1/di
Relates focal length (f), object distance (do), and image distance (di). Magnification m = -di/do. Real images have positive di.
Bernoulli's Flow Equation
P + 1/2*r*v^2 + r*g*h = const
Conservation of energy for fluids. As flow speed (v) increases in narrow areas (continuity), pressure (P) decreases.
Ohm's Electrical Law
V = I * R
Voltage (V) equals Current (I) times Resistance (R). Resistors in series sum directly (R = R1+R2). Resistors in parallel sum inversely (1/R = 1/R1 + 1/R2).

CARS Timing & Passages

The 9-Passage 90-Minute Rules
Allocate exactly 10 minutes per passage. Do not exceed 4 minutes reading a passage. You cannot read word-for-word details; read to map the structure (Author Thesis, Paragraph Functions, Key Shift words like 'However').
Avoid the Extreme Options Trap
Answers that contain absolute qualifiers like "always", "never", "only", or "absolutely" are almost always incorrect. The MCAT rewards moderate, nuanced reasoning.
The "Main Idea" Fallback
When struggling with a question, select the answer choice that aligns most closely with the author's primary arguments. Eliminate choices that are true facts but fail to address the actual prompt context.

Active Recall & Mock Exams

Spaced Repetition Schedule
Review your flashcard decks daily. Space review periods of hard concepts (1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 2 weeks) to move memorizations into long-term structures.
Simulate Real Testing Conditions
Complete full-length exams starting at 8:00 AM. Wear earplugs, take exactly the scheduled breaks, and avoid checking notes. Standardizing test environments mitigates testing anxiety.
The Diagnostic Error Notebook
Write down every question you got wrong or guessed. Categorize them: "Content Gap", "Misreading", or "Logical Error". Rereading this log prior to study sessions blocks recurring mistakes.
